Orbital-debris risk model & compensation framework

A risk score for objects in Earth orbit, and a fund priced by it.

This model assigns each object in orbit an Orbital Risk Score (ORS) computed from public catalogue data, and defines a contribution to a proposed Orbital Debris Compensation Fund (ODCF) proportional to that score. The score and the contribution are recomputed live in the browser from documented parameters.

What this is

The ORS combines three factors into a single normalised value between 0 and 1: an object's potential to generate debris, its orbital persistence, and the collision density of its orbital region. Contributions to the fund are levied on new launches in proportion to the risk each adds.

The model is transparent and parametric: every constant is written up on the Methodology page, and the datasets are all on the Data page. It is calibrated on real catalogue data but is not a conjunction-assessment system; figures are indicative.

Origin

This model and website operationalise Orbital Stewardship: A Framework for a Collective Liability Fund to Combat Space Debris. The paper wasn't getting published :/, so I just decided to build the model anyway.

I am Janav, a fifth-year law student at the National Law School of India University. My interest in space law developed through my participation in the 2024 Manfred Lachs Space Law Moot. It was then that I became aware of the scale of the orbital debris problem and the legal/technical lacunae of an effective liability regime that addresses damage caused by space debris. The full paper is available on the Space Debris page.

Full disclosure: the website (not the model) has been built with Claude Code.
