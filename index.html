Orbital-debris risk model & compensation framework

A risk score for objects in Earth orbit, and a fund priced by it.

This model assigns each object in orbit an Orbital Risk Score (ORS) computed from public catalogue data, and defines a contribution to a proposed Orbital Debris Compensation Fund (ODCF) proportional to that score. The score and the contribution are recomputed live in the browser from documented parameters.

What this is

The ORS combines three factors into a single normalised value between 0 and 1: an object's potential to generate debris, its orbital persistence, and the collision density of its orbital region. Contributions to the fund are levied on new launches in proportion to the risk each adds.

The model is transparent and parametric: every constant is published on the Methodology page and every dataset is available on the Data page. It is calibrated on real catalogue data but is not a conjunction-assessment system; figures are indicative.

Origin

This model and website operationalise Orbital Stewardship: A Framework for a Collective Liability Fund to Combat Space Debris, a paper by Janav Arun. Janav is a fifth-year law student at the National Law School of India University. His interest in space law developed through his participation in the 2024 Manfred Lachs Space Law Moot Court Competition, where he became aware of the scale of the orbital debris problem. The full paper is available on the Space Debris page.
